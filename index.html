Projects

ChemECar

ChemECar is a fully functioning model car that is purely powered by chemical reactions. As part of the team I was a mechanical design Engineer and tasked to construct CAD models of key components of the car on Solidworks and then assemble these components using the assembly features. When designing these components we had to consider how they would interact with other components of the vehicle that were being designed by other teams. We would then 3D print these components and then finaly assemble them in time for the competion held in China. This project taught me how to work in a group environment efficiently and to prioritise delivery time to ensure the project is delivered on time.

Gridfinity System

The Gridfinity system is a highly customisable printable modular storage system designed by Zach Freedman to organise workspaces. I took this opportunity as a learning experience for fabriacting 3D components using a 3D printer and CAD as well as to clear up any clutter in my workspace. The base plates were standard 42 by 42mm. These were created and then printed using my personal printer. The housing for the components were however completed using Fusion 360. These parts had to be modelled using the constaints of size and considerations were made on the perfect fit. These fiels were then optimised for 3D printing to reduce time, filament usage and most importantly cost. This project taught me design for manufacture and optimising prints for cost, time and print quality. This project also enhanced my CAD skills as I was making the moulds for the items I was priting.

Solar Sunray

A solar-powered car was created using four panels: two small and two large. Electrical optimisations were implemented to ensure the car could operate under all environmental conditions experienced on Earth, including sunny, cloudy, and rainy weather. In total, eight switches were installed to control the series and parallel configurations of the panels, allowing the electrical system to be optimised for varying conditions. Gear-switching technology was also developed; however, due to time constraints, this system could not be implemented in the final design. This was one of my first projects that I did in university in a team environment and it gave me great insight on how to contribute in a team with different ideas.
